The Starter Climb

A gentle, no-pressure plan built around exactly what you have right now: a StairMaster, dumbbells, and 3 days a week. Every session sculpts the two areas you care about most — a strong, toned back and rounder glutes — while the cardio quietly burns in the background.

Your cardio climbs like a staircase

Things that make this work

🍑 How glutes get rounder

Square glutes round out from two things: building the muscle (your bridges, RDLs, and sumo squats) and losing a little fat over time. Squeeze hard at the top of every rep — lazy reps build nothing.

💪 How back rolls fade

Honest truth: you can't burn fat from one spot. The rolls fade as overall weight comes down, while your rows and flys build the toned, defined shape that shows up underneath. Both halves of this plan are working on it.

🏋️ Pick weights that feel easy-ish

You should finish each set feeling like you had 2–3 reps left in you. When that feels true two workouts in a row, add reps first, then weight.

🍽️ Food does the heavy lifting

For losing ~25 lbs, what you eat matters more than any workout. Start simple: protein at every meal, water instead of sugary drinks. No crash diets — slow is what sticks.

📅 Consistency beats intensity

9–12 workouts this month is the entire goal. A "bad" workout where you showed up counts as a full win.

📈 Track wins that aren't the scale

More StairMaster minutes, heavier dumbbells, jeans fitting differently, better posture in photos — these show up before the scale moves.
