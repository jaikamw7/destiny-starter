The Starter Plate

Simple, filling food that keeps you in a gentle calorie deficit while fueling your workouts. Every meal has swaps, nothing is banned, and nothing requires fancy cooking. This is the half of the plan that does most of the work.

Your daily food budget

About 1,800 calories a day puts you in a comfortable deficit — roughly 0.5–1 lb a week on desk-only days and more on 10k-step days, without ever feeling starved or low-energy for the gym. Here's how it splits across the day:

Breakfast~400 cal
Lunch~500 cal
Dinner~550 cal
Snacks~350 cal
Why not eat way less? Crash dieting tanks your energy, makes workouts miserable, and almost always rebounds. Eating enough — especially protein — keeps your metabolism humming and protects the muscle you're building. Slow is what sticks.

Each slot has a main pick plus swaps at roughly the same calories and protein. Mix and match freely — eat the same breakfast all week or rotate, whatever's easiest. Portions are starting points, not laws.

Things that make this work

🥩 Protein is the cheat code

Aim for 130g a day minimum, 150g on a great day. That's matched to your lean mass — the muscle protein actually feeds — which is what the research scales protein to. It keeps you full, protects the muscle your workouts build, and burns more calories to digest than carbs or fat do.

👟 Your 10k steps are a superpower

With a desk job, your step days burn ~300–400 more calories than office-chair days — that's the difference between a slow week and a great one. Steps are the easiest lever you have; protect that habit.

⚖️ One bad day changes nothing

Weight loss is decided by your weekly average, not any single day. Overdid it at a birthday dinner? Just go back to the plan at the next meal — no punishing, no "starting over Monday."

🍟 Eating out survival

Pick a protein-forward main (grilled anything, burrito bowl, burger without fries), drink water, and don't stress the rest. One restaurant meal fits in the budget just fine.

📉 What progress looks like

Expect roughly 0.5–1.5 lbs a week on average depending on your step count, but the scale zigzags daily from water and hormones. Weigh weekly at most, same day and time, and judge the monthly trend.

🛒 Keep it boring on purpose

Repeating the same 2–3 breakfasts and lunches every week is a feature, not a failure. Fewer decisions = easier consistency. Save variety for dinner.

😋 Hungry between meals?

First, water — thirst loves disguising itself as hunger. Still hungry 15 minutes later? Have a protein snack. Real hunger is fine to feed; this plan has room.
